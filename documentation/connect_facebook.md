# Facebook Messenger Connection Guide (Beta)

This guide provides a comprehensive, step-by-step walkthrough to connect your Facebook Page / Messenger channel to OpenSquadron via the Meta Graph API.

---

## Prerequisites
Before you begin, ensure you have:
1. A **Meta Developer Account** (register at [developers.facebook.com](https://developers.facebook.com)).
2. A **Meta Business Portfolio** (Business Manager account) or a personal profile linked to a Facebook Page.
3. A **Facebook Page** (you must be an Admin of the page you wish to connect).

---

## Step 1: Create a Meta Developer App
1. Go to the [Meta App Dashboard](https://developers.facebook.com/apps) and click **Create App**.
2. Select **Other** -> **Business** (or Choose the **Messenger** type if presented with simplified options).
3. Provide an App Name (e.g., `OpenSquadron Messenger Bot`) and link it to your Meta Business Portfolio or keep it personal.
4. Click **Create app**.

---

## Step 2: Set Up the Messenger Product
1. Scroll down to the list of products inside your new App Dashboard and click **Set up** on the **Messenger** product.
2. Under the **App Settings** / **Messenger** menu, navigate to **Facebook Pages**.
3. Click **Add or remove Pages** and authorize the Meta app to manage your specific Facebook Page.
4. Once added, click **Generate token** next to your Page name.
5. Save this **Page Access Token** somewhere secure; you will need it in OpenSquadron.

---

## Step 3: Retrieve Key Meta Credentials
You will need the following credentials from your Meta Developer Portal to link the connection:

1. **Page ID**: The unique numeric identifier of your Facebook Page. You can find this in your Page's "About" section or under the Pages section in the Meta App Developer Dashboard (e.g., `104829374019283`).
2. **App ID**: Located at the top bar of your Meta Developer App Dashboard (e.g., `582910394019284`).
3. **App Secret**: Navigate to **App settings** -> **Basic** in the developer sidebar, click **Show** next to *App Secret*, and copy it.
4. **Page Access Token**: The token generated in **Step 2** for your page.

---

## Step 4: Configure the Connection in OpenSquadron
1. Log in to your OpenSquadron Admin Dashboard.
2. Navigate to **Connect Facebook** under the *Bot Channels* dropdown menu (or go to `/admin/facebook/connect` directly).
3. Fill out the **Add New Facebook Connection** wizard:
   * **Connection Label**: Enter a user-friendly name (e.g., `Facebook Support Bot`, `Messenger Sales Line`).
   * **Facebook Page ID**: Paste your retrieved *Page ID*.
   * **Facebook App ID**: Paste your retrieved *App ID*.
   * **Facebook App Secret**: Paste your *App Secret* (used securely for validating webhook signature payloads).
   * **Page Access Token**: Paste the generated *Page Access Token*.
4. Click **Connect Page**. OpenSquadron will validate the credentials against the Meta Graph API and securely save the connection under your multi-tenant workspace.

---

## Step 5: Configure Inbound Webhooks in Meta
To receive subscriber messages in real-time in your shared inbox, configure the secure webhook:

1. After saving, the OpenSquadron configuration dashboard displays **Wizard Step 2: Map Webhook Endpoints**.
2. Copy the auto-generated **Webhook Callback URL** (e.g., `https://yourdomain.com/webhook/facebook`).
   > [!WARNING]
   > Meta requires an **HTTPS** callback URL. For local developer testing, you must tunnel your local port via a service like Cloudflare Tunnel or ngrok to get a secure public HTTPS endpoint.
3. Copy the **Verify Token** generated by OpenSquadron for this connection.
4. Go back to your **Meta App Dashboard** -> **Messenger** -> **Webhook Settings** (or **App Dashboard** -> **Webhooks**).
5. Click **Edit** or **Configure Webhook**:
   * Paste the copied **Webhook Callback URL** into the *Callback URL* field.
   * Paste the copied **Verify Token** into the *Verify Token* field.
   * Click **Verify and save**.
6. **CRITICAL**: Once verified, click **Manage** or **Add Subscriptions** for the webhook. Find the **messages** and **messaging_postbacks** rows and click **Subscribe**. Without subscribing to these events, Meta will not forward incoming customer messages and quick-replies to your server.

---

## Step 6: Verify and Test the Setup
1. **Developer Sandbox Notice**:
   > [!IMPORTANT]
   > While your Facebook Developer App is in *Development Mode* (not yet approved via Meta App Review), only App Admins, Developers, and Roles/Testers registered inside your Meta Developer Dashboard can message the page and receive automated replies. Once you are ready for the public, you must submit the app for App Review and request the `pages_messaging` permission.
2. Open your shared inbox in OpenSquadron.
3. Send a message to your Facebook Page from an authorized tester's Facebook account.
4. Verify that the message instantly appears in your shared inbox:
   * The system will automatically create a new subscriber profile (displaying `PSID: <Page Scoped User ID>` until updated or custom attributes are set).
   * A **Facebook (Beta)** badge will be shown next to their name in the sidebar and in the active conversation header.
   * **Exemption from 24h Warning**: Facebook Messenger operators utilize the standard `HUMAN_AGENT` message tag. OpenSquadron bypasses the 24-hour lockdown window for Facebook subscribers, allowing you to respond freely up to 7 days from the last message without templates.
5. Send a reply from your shared inbox and verify it is received in the sender's Messenger app.
