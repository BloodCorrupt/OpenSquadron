# How to Connect Facebook & Instagram to OpenSquadron

OpenSquadron uses a **Single Meta Developer App** to handle connections for both Facebook Pages and Instagram Professional Accounts. This means you only need to create ONE app in the Meta Developer Portal, and you can reuse its credentials for all your bots.

## Step 1: Create a Meta Developer App
1. Go to [Meta for Developers](https://developers.facebook.com/) and log in with your Facebook account.
2. Click on **My Apps** in the top right corner.
3. Click the **Create App** button.
4. Select **Other** as the use case and click **Next**.
5. Select **Business** as the app type and click **Next**.
6. Fill in the **App Name** (e.g., `OpenSquadron App`) and **Contact Email**. Choose a Business Account if you have one, then click **Create App**.

## Step 2: Configure Basic Settings
1. In the left sidebar, navigate to **App Settings** > **Basic**.
2. Locate your **App ID** and **App Secret**. You will need these later to connect the app to OpenSquadron.
3. Scroll down to the **Privacy Policy URL** and **Terms of Service URL**. You must provide valid URLs here for your app to go live.
4. Add an **App Icon** (1024x1024) to make your app look professional.

## Step 3: Add Products
1. In the left sidebar, click **Add Product** (or the **+** icon next to Products).
2. Find **Facebook Login for Business** and click **Set Up**.
3. Find **Messenger** and click **Set Up**.
4. Find **Instagram Graph API** and click **Set Up** (Note: this might be under a combined "Messenger and Instagram" product depending on your Meta UI version).

## Step 4: Configure Facebook Login
1. In the left sidebar under **Facebook Login for Business**, click **Settings**.
2. Ensure **Valid OAuth Redirect URIs** contains BOTH of your OpenSquadron callback URLs:
   * **Facebook Callback:** `https://your-opensquadron-domain.com/facebook/callback`
   * **Instagram Callback:** `https://your-opensquadron-domain.com/instagram/callback`
3. Save changes.

## Step 5: Input Credentials in OpenSquadron
1. Log in to your OpenSquadron dashboard.
2. Navigate to **Admin Panel > Meta App Settings**.
3. Copy your **App ID** and **App Secret** from the Meta Developer Portal.
4. Paste them into the OpenSquadron settings page and click **Save Settings**.
5. Note the **Verify Token** generated by OpenSquadron. You will need it for Webhook setup.

## Step 6: Configure Webhooks in Meta
1. In the Meta Developer Portal, under the **Messenger** product, click **Settings**.
2. Scroll to the **Webhooks** section and click **Add Callback URL**.
3. Enter your OpenSquadron Facebook Webhook URL:
   * `https://your-opensquadron-domain.com/webhook/facebook`
4. Enter the **Verify Token** you got from OpenSquadron.
5. Select the following subscription fields: `messages`, `messaging_postbacks`, `messaging_optins`, `message_deliveries`, `message_reads`.
6. Go back to the **Webhooks** section, but this time select **Instagram** from the dropdown menu (if available) or go to **Instagram Graph API > Webhooks**.
7. Enter your OpenSquadron Instagram Webhook URL:
   * `https://your-opensquadron-domain.com/webhook/instagram`
8. Enter the SAME **Verify Token**.
9. Select the following subscription fields: `messages`, `messaging_postbacks`, `comments`.

## Step 7: Connect Your Pages and Accounts
In OpenSquadron, you must connect Facebook and Instagram separately depending on what you want to automate.

**For Facebook:**
1. Go to **Connect Bot > Connect Facebook** from the top navigation dropdown.
2. Click the connect button, log into Meta, and select the Facebook Pages you want to connect.
3. After returning, select a Page from the list and click **Connect Page**. OpenSquadron will automatically subscribe it to your webhooks.

**For Instagram (IMPORTANT):**
1. Your Instagram account **must** be a Professional (Business/Creator) account.
2. Your Instagram account **must** be linked to a Facebook Page that you are an Admin of.
3. Go to **Connect Bot > Connect Instagram** from the top navigation dropdown.
4. Click the connect button, log into Meta, and select the Instagram Professional Accounts (and their linked Facebook Pages) you want to connect.
5. After returning, select an Account from the list and click **Connect Instagram Account**.

## Step 8: App Roles & Instagram Testers (DEVELOPMENT MODE ONLY)
If your app is in **Development Mode**, only designated testers can send messages that your webhook will receive. 
**If you do not do this, incoming Instagram DMs will be silently ignored by Meta.**

1. In the Meta Developer Portal, go to **App Roles > Roles**.
2. Scroll down to the **Instagram Testers** section and click **Add Instagram Testers**.
3. Enter the Instagram username of the account that will be *sending* test messages (i.e., your personal Instagram account testing the bot).
4. The user must accept the invite in the Instagram app (Settings & activity -> Website permissions -> App invites).
5. (Note: The Facebook account that created the Meta App automatically has Admin access, but you *still* need to add the sender's Instagram account as an Instagram Tester for Instagram DMs to work).

## Step 9: Go Live
Before your app can be used by the general public, you need to switch it from **Development** mode to **Live** mode.
1. At the top of the Meta Developer dashboard, toggle the switch from **App Mode: Development** to **Live**.
2. If prompted, complete the Data Use Checkup and Business Verification.

**Note on Permissions:** For full automation capabilities with public users in Live mode, your Meta App requires **Advanced Access** for certain permissions (specifically `pages_messaging`, `instagram_manage_messages`, and `instagram_basic`). You can request these under **App Review > Permissions and Features**.
